About
Future School converts the Korean Pavilion into a time-based performative platform where thought leaders and engaged citizens from around the world assemble to focus on three critical subjects: diaspora, climate crisis and innovation
An incubator for radical thinking, Future School activates community in the formation of new collaborations. It does so by reimagining the archetypal educational institution as a site for process, connection, and exchange - a meeting place to address imminent global challenges.
Together with contributing architects, educators, artists, theorists, and advocates, participants are invited to engage in acts of unlearning and relearning through Future School events, such as workshops, roundtables, lectures, performances, and exhibitions.
The aspiration to foster cross-disciplinary, interactive engagement of all kinds is reflected in the flexible use of the Pavilion site, specifically the inclusion of informal domestic spaces that gently but resolutely dissolve the boundaries between learning and living. Incorporated into Future School, for example, is an open kitchen, a circular lounge, and a retreat room constructed entirely in “hanji,” a traditional, handmade Korean paper.
Parallel programming runs concurrently at Future School’s Seoul campus throughout the duration of the Biennale in Venice, with live broadcasts between locations and participants plugging in from other cities and live projects environment. Visitors can participate in over 50 programmes exploring different issues, from cooling urban environments and the futurology of schools, to innovative spatial interventions and borders as spaces of integration.
